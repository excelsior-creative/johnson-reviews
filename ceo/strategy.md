# Strategy — Johnson Reviews

**One sentence.** Johnson Reviews is Brandon Johnson's personal brand as a
family travel blogger and restaurant reviewer — a trusted voice for
traveling families looking for real, specific reviews of restaurants,
hotels, and experiences.

## Who this is for

Primary: **Other traveling families in Southern California and the places
they go** — Orange County locals considering a restaurant, families
planning Disneyland trips, parents looking for genuine "is this place
kid-friendly?" signal without hype.

Secondary: travel-curious general audience who trust Brandon's voice and
follow for the writing, not just the next vacation decision.

## What we are

- A real family's real experiences. Every review reflects an actual
  Johnson family visit.
- Restaurant + travel + family experiences. Mid-range and casual as often
  as upscale. Disney, Starbucks, chain diners, hidden gems — all in
  scope.
- Southern California as home base, broader travel (Vegas, Puerto
  Vallarta, Hollywood FL, Richmond, etc.) as expansion.
- Honest. Warm when earned, critical when warranted. Never cruel.
- Written in Brandon's voice. See `voice.md`.

## What we are NOT

- A fine-dining critic site. We review Michelin meals when the Johnsons
  actually have them, but that's not our lane and the voice is not
  stuffy-critic.
- A listicle / SEO-farm travel blog ("17 MUST-SEE SPOTS IN…").
- An affiliate marketing vehicle. Affiliates are downstream of trust.
- An influencer / press-trip / comped-content operation. Independent first.
- Anything generic enough that you could swap the author's name and not
  notice.

## The bets

1. **Authority compounds.** Consistent, specific, honest reviews at
   volume — with proper schema + internal linking — become a trusted
   voice over time. Google rewards that. Readers reward that.
2. **Specificity is the moat.** "The host walked out from the kitchen
   with a plate of something he hadn't mentioned" is the asset. Generic
   travel-blog copy is commodity.
3. **The bottleneck is Brandon's time, not his experiences.** The
   Johnsons go places constantly. The review backlog is large. The
   workflow that cheaply converts memory → publishable review is the
   highest-leverage infrastructure we can build.
4. **Family privacy > reach.** Kids never get identified by full
   name/school/home. Geotags scrubbed. This constrains some content;
   that's fine.

## Priority order (first weeks)

1. Ship the site redesign per `/reference/design/`.
2. Build the conversational review intake workflow.
3. Publish reviews and grow the library.
4. SEO and authority — schema, internal linking, category/location hubs.
5. Monetization. Lower priority until authority is established.

## Open strategic questions

- **Voice vs. reference-design tone mismatch.** The reference design
  copy ("The Art of the Critique," "uncompromising palate," "Michelin
  II," "Valet Parking") reads haute-cuisine-critic, which is not
  Brandon. Is the reference only a *visual* direction, with copy meant
  to be Brandon's family-travel voice, or is the brand actually
  pivoting upmarket? → Ask Brandon. Answer shapes nearly everything.
