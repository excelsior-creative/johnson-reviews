# Strategy — Johnson Reviews

_Last updated: 2026-04-20 (first nightly run; treat as v0 — confirm with Brandon)_

## What Johnson Reviews is

A personal-brand publication built on Brandon Johnson's real experiences as
a family traveler and restaurant goer. Authority comes from volume + honesty
+ specificity, not from manufactured glamour. Brandon is already a
Google Local Guide Level 10 with 500+ reviews and ~132M photo views — the
authority is already partially earned, the site monetizes and amplifies it.

## What Johnson Reviews is NOT

- A Michelin-style fine-dining critic site. (The current visual reference
  reads that way; the *copy* must not.)
- A breathless "MUST-VISIT!" listicle factory.
- An aspirational-Instagram travel blog with stock-photo wanderlust energy.
- A site that fabricates experiences. Every review reflects an actual
  Johnson family visit.
- Brandon writing alone in real time. The CEO/agent does the writing
  scaffold; Brandon owns voice and sign-off.

## Audience (best-guess v0 — confirm with Brandon)

Primary, in order of believed weight:
1. **Other traveling families in SoCal / OC** searching for "is this place
   stroller-friendly," "kid menu at X," "worth the drive to Dana Point for
   brunch?" High commercial intent, low BS tolerance.
2. **Locals (OC, LA, San Diego)** looking for trusted restaurant
   recommendations from someone who actually eats out a lot.
3. **Family travelers planning trips to destinations the Johnsons cover**
   (Vegas, Disney, Puerto Vallarta, etc.) — looking for ground-truth from
   another family, not from a travel agency.

Secondary:
- Brands and tourism boards looking at Brandon's reach (Google Local
  Guide profile + site + future social) for partnership opportunities.

## Positioning bet

**"The honest local who travels well and brings the family."** The
differentiator vs. generic travel blogs is specificity (real names, real
moments, real frustrations) and the family lens (other parents trust
parents). The differentiator vs. Yelp/Google reviews is voice + depth +
context (a 600-word essay beats a 4-sentence Yelp post when someone is
deciding whether to drive 40 minutes to a place).

## What we're betting on

1. **Brandon's existing Google Local Guide footprint** is an underused
   asset. There are 500+ existing reviews on Google. A subset of those
   become full-length blog posts on the site, with photos, schema, and
   internal linking — and they should outrank the Google review for the
   same query in many cases.
2. **Conversational intake** is the bottleneck-killer. Brandon talks,
   Claude writes, Brandon redlines, ship. 60+ legacy reviews already
   exist as WordPress mirror in `reference/` — the migration alone is
   weeks of content.
3. **Schema + local SEO** wins family-travel + restaurant queries.
   `LocalBusiness`/`Restaurant`/`Review` schema with real ratings and
   real visit dates is enforceable trust signal. Most family-travel
   blogs do this badly.

## Anti-bets

- Not betting on going viral on TikTok or Instagram in the next quarter.
  Social is a downstream amplifier, not the primary acquisition channel.
- Not betting on press-trip reciprocity yet. Disclosure obligations and
  voice integrity matter more than free meals at this stage.
- Not betting on a paid newsletter or membership in the first 6 months.
  Authority needs to compound first.

## Unknowns / questions for Brandon

1. Audience — is the OC/SoCal-family lens the right one, or are we
   primarily a national family travel site that happens to be based
   in OC?
2. What's the ad/affiliate posture? Open to Mediavine / display ads
   once traffic supports it?
3. Is monetization a 2026 thing or a 2027 thing? Drives how aggressively
   we instrument.
4. Are there partner brands or tourism boards Brandon already has
   relationships with that we should preserve / not poach?
