# Strategy

## What Johnson Reviews is

A trusted, opinionated, *family-shaped* lens on travel and restaurants.
Brandon and his family actually go places, actually eat there, and
write it down. The site exists so other families — and curious diners
— can borrow that judgment.

## What it isn't

- Not a fine-dining critic blog. Visual design is editorial-luxe, but
  the content is family-real.
- Not a generic SEO travel listicle farm. No "12 Things You MUST DO
  in San Diego."
- Not an Instagram aesthetic mood board. Photos serve the review,
  not the other way around.
- Not a press-trip-driven content machine (yet). Sponsorships and
  comped experiences require disclosure; Brandon decides each one.

## Audience (in priority order)

1. **Other traveling families** in Southern California, Las Vegas,
   and the family-vacation circuit (Disneyland, San Diego, Vegas,
   PV, Hawaii, etc.). They want trustworthy answers to "is this
   actually good with kids?"
2. **Local diners** searching for a specific restaurant in OC / LA /
   SD. Strong local-SEO opportunity — many of the historical reviews
   are SoCal restaurants.
3. **General travel-curious readers** who'll find us via search and
   stay for the voice.

Aspirational: brand recognition in the family-travel space such that
hotels/restaurants invite us proactively (with full disclosure).

## Positioning

> Real trips. Honest reviews. Written by a dad who actually went.

The visual chrome is luxury-editorial (per the redesign reference).
The content voice is warm, specific, family-inclusive, never
breathless. The juxtaposition — luxe presentation, lived-in voice —
is the differentiator.

## What we're betting on

- **Trust compounds.** Honest reviews of small SoCal restaurants
  beat hot-take international fluff for SEO and reader loyalty.
- **Specificity wins.** "Stroller fits between the patio tables"
  outperforms "great atmosphere."
- **Kids show up tastefully.** Family is the lens, not the punchline.
  Privacy-first photo handling is non-negotiable.
- **Authority is local before it's global.** Win SoCal first.

## Open strategic questions (for Brandon)

- Geographic focus: are we leaning into OC/LA/SD as the home base,
  or treating the site as truly national from day one?
- Restaurant vs. travel mix: roughly 60/40 in current backlog —
  is that the long-run target?
- Affiliate posture: do we pursue Booking.com / Expedia / restaurant
  reservation affiliates now, or wait until traffic justifies it?
- Press-trip / comped-meal policy: blanket no, case-by-case, or
  open with disclosure?
