# Intake Prompt — Review Interview Template

_Version 1.0 — Created 2026-04-26. Use this every time Brandon initiates a new review._

## Purpose

This is the script the CEO agent uses when Brandon says "we went to X" or "I want to write up Y." Paste these questions, adapt them to the specific venue type, and wait for his answers. Don't draft until you have enough to write a specific, honest review.

---

## Step 1 — Confirm the Basics

Ask:

> **"Quick intake for [PLACE] — before I draft, I need a few specifics so I don't invent details:**
>
> 1. **When did you go?** (approximate is fine — month/year)
> 2. **Who came?** Just you, with the whole family, date night, group?
> 3. **What did you order?** (Specific dish names help a lot — even partial names)
> 4. **What was the standout moment?** (The specific thing you'd tell a friend — a dish, a view, something the kids did, something unexpected)
> 5. **What didn't work?** (If anything — could be parking, wait, a dish that fell flat, service hiccup)
> 6. **Would you go back?** (Yes, probably, no)
> 7. **Any logistics worth noting?** (Parking, reservation recommended?, kid-friendly?, stroller ok?, wait time, price tier)"

---

## Step 2 — Venue-Type Follow-ups

Adapt based on what Brandon tells you. Add 1–3 of these depending on venue:

**Restaurants:**
- "What did the kids eat? Did they have a kids' menu or did they just share?"
- "Bar-seating or table? Inside or patio?"
- "What was the price per person approximately?"
- "Reservation needed, or walk-in?"

**Hotels/Resorts:**
- "What room type? How many nights?"
- "What made it worth it vs. what could have been better?"
- "Best thing about the property — the pool, the room, the service, location?"
- "Would you book it again at the same rate? At a higher rate?"

**Theme Parks/Entertainment:**
- "What did you actually do / ride / see? Any must-dos?"
- "How long did you stay? Crowds?"
- "What worked for your kids' ages? What didn't?"
- "Genie+, Lightning Lane, or standby? Was it worth it?"

**Shopping:**
- "What was the context — destination shopping or wandered in?"
- "Anything you bought that was worth mentioning?"

---

## Step 3 — Photo Check

Ask:
> "Do you have photos? Even a Google Photos album link works. I'll suggest which images to use where. (If kids are in the shots, let me know and I'll strip geotag metadata before publish.)"

---

## Step 4 — Draft

Once Brandon has answered Steps 1–2:
- Write a 400–700 word draft per `/ceo/voice.md`.
- Structure: hook → what we ordered/did → who it's for → what worked → what didn't → logistics → bottom line.
- Post to Slack C0AHR2NJ361 as a code block or Google Doc link.
- Label it clearly: `DRAFT — [Place] — not published`.

---

## Notes on When to Ask vs. When to Infer

**Never infer or make up:**
- What they ordered
- What the kids ate
- Whether they'd go back
- Specific prices, wait times, reservations
- Anything involving the family's experience

**OK to infer from context:**
- Category (restaurant vs. hotel)
- Geographic context from the name/location
- General seasonal references if Brandon mentions a date

---

## What NOT to Ask

- Don't ask for a rating score unless Brandon volunteers one. Ratings may come later as a site feature.
- Don't ask Brandon to write anything. Your job is to draft it; his job is to react and correct.
- Don't ask 15 questions at once. The intro list above is the max. Follow-ups come after his first reply.
