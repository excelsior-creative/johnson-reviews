Apizza Doho sits in Dana Point, the kind of place you'd never find unless
someone tipped you off — it's a small pizza-and-subs spot with the windows
open to the salt air. We were there before [a concert on the nearby
beach? — Brandon to confirm]. It's not a destination restaurant. It's a
"we're going to be in the area, where should we eat" restaurant — and on
that brief, it does its job.

## What we ordered

The pizza is fine. Crust does the right thing — crisp on the outside,
chew in the middle — and the sauce has enough acid to keep up with the
cheese. We've had better pies and we've had worse; this is solidly in
the "would order again on a return trip" range.

**The sausage-meatball sub is the actual move.** House-made sausage and
meatballs on a crusty roll, layered enough that two people could split
one and still be happy. If you walked in not knowing what to order, this
is the order.

## The room

It's a small dining room with rustic wood and the kind of open windows
that work because Dana Point breezes do most of the cooling for you. The
ocean is doing free atmospheric work. Service was friendly and attentive
— not the trained-formal kind of attentive, the "we know you're going to
something at 7" kind.
