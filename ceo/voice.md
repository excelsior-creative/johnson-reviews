## Voice principles (working draft)

### Treat him as a dad, not a critic.
He's writing for other parents. The lens is "would we go back; would we
take the kids; what should you know before you go." Not "does the
reduction honor the terroir."

### Specificity over superlatives.
"The host walked out from the kitchen with a plate of something he
hadn't mentioned." Good.
"This place is a MUST VISIT!" Bad.
Name the dish. Name the kid. Name the moment. If there's no specific
memory, ask for one or don't write the paragraph.

### Warm when earned, honest when not.
A place that disappointed gets an honest review with context — "the
service was distracted, the food arrived cold, we wouldn't go back" —
not "this place SUCKS." Constructive beats mean. Brandon doesn't do
mean.

### Write like a person, not a travel-blog SEO farm.
No "Nestled in the heart of…" No "A hidden gem awaits." No
"uncompromising palate" / "culinary excellence" / "a symphony of
flavors" / "every bite is art" clichés.

### Use "we," not "I."
It's the Johnson family, not the Johnson-critic-essayist. "We sat at a
corner table." "The kids made a beeline for the bread basket." Plural
first-person.

### Family is in the frame, but the kids are protected.
They're there in the story — what they ate, what made them laugh, what
they complained about — but not by full name, not tagged to specific
schools or home addresses, not shown in photos where geotags reveal
location.

### Short paragraphs. Rhythm matters.
Mix sentence lengths. Let a short sentence carry weight after a long
one. Read it out loud: if it sounds like a press release, rewrite.

## Things Brandon would never say (working list)

- "Must visit!" / "Must try!" / "Absolute MUST."
- "Foodie paradise."
- "Hidden gem" (unless actually justified and said with self-awareness).
- "Nestled" / "Tucked away" / "Imbued with."
- "Culinary journey / experience / symphony / alchemy."
- "The epitome of perfection."
- "Every bite was a revelation."
- "Uncompromising palate" / "rigorous analysis" / "standard of taste"
  (this is currently on the live Hero; flag to remove).
- Anything that sounds like an AI-generated listicle title
  ("Unveiling…", "Experiencing…", "Discovering Hidden…").

## Structural defaults for a review (until Brandon overrides)

- **Lede** (1 paragraph): the moment that stuck. Set the scene. One
  specific, sensory detail. Who was there.
- **Context** (1–2 paragraphs): where it is, why you went, what the
  occasion was.
- **The food / the experience** (2–4 paragraphs): what you had, what
  surprised you, what disappointed you, what the kids thought.
- **The logistics for other families** (1 paragraph): parking,
  reservations, stroller-friendly, kid menu, wait times, price tier.
- **The verdict** (1–2 sentences + rating): would you go back, who is
  this for.
