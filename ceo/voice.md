## Default voice dial

- **Warm, specific, honest.** The baseline register is "telling
  a friend about dinner last weekend", not "filing a review
  with a magazine."
- **"We" more than "I"**, because it's a family brand. Use "I"
  when Brandon is clearly the one who tried a thing ("I
  ordered the prime rib"). Default to "we" for the visit.
- **Past tense, concrete moments.** "We showed up around 6:30
  and the patio was already full" > "This spot gets busy."
- **Short paragraphs, occasional one-liners for punch.**
- **Sentence rhythm is mixed** — longer sentence, then a short
  one. Not every sentence should be the same length.
- **Light humor, dry rather than wacky.** No exclamation points
  except when they're genuinely earned by surprise.

## Banned vocabulary and tics (generic-blog voice detectors)

- "A MUST VISIT" / "must-try" / "must-have"

## How to describe food

- Name the specific dish. "The margherita" not "pizza".
- Describe texture and execution, not emotion. "The crust had
  real chew and a little char on the edge" > "absolutely
  divine".
- Note portion size and value where it matters — families are
  cost-conscious.
- If Brandon hated something, say what was actually wrong with
  it (cold, over-salted, tiny portion), not how disappointing
  it was.

## How to describe a place

- Lead with the logistics a family needs: parking,
  reservations, wait time, stroller/booster situation, kids
  menu, noise level, whether it gets loud at night.
- Then the vibe: who else was there, what the space felt like,
  whether the kids were comfortable.
- Then the food / main event.
- Then the verdict: would we go back, who would we send here,
  is it worth the drive.

## How to handle disappointments

- Never mean. "Service was off the night we went" > "the
  staff were clueless."
- Always include context — was it a busy Friday, was the
  restaurant understaffed, was it opening week?
- Say what would have to change for us to go back.
- If the issue is a one-off, say so. If it's structural
  (cramped, overpriced, bad acoustics), say that plainly.

## How to compliment

- Specific over superlative. "The host came back ten minutes
  later with a plate of churros we hadn't ordered, on the
  house" beats "the service was incredible."
- Attribution where deserved. Name the server, the chef, the
  host, if Brandon remembers them.

## Family mentions

- The family shows up naturally, not as a gimmick. Don't shoehorn
  "the kids loved it" into every review.
- Kid reactions are useful data: if a place passed the kid
  test, say which kid, which dish, and why it worked.
- **Privacy**: no full kid names, no school references, no
  home-area photos, strip geotags from kid photos. See
  `photos.md`.

## Structure template (provisional)

```
H1: [Place name] — [one-line editorial tag]
Lede paragraph: when we went, who was with us, the 30-second
take.
Logistics block: parking, reservations, kid menu, stroller,
price tier, hours, address.
The visit: 2–4 paragraphs of concrete moments.
The food / main event: what we ordered, what stood out.
The verdict: would we go back, who it's for, one sentence.
```
