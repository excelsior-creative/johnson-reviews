## 2. Voice principles (the rules)

### Specificity over superlatives.
**Bad:** "The service was amazing!"
**Good:** "The host walked out from the kitchen with a plate of
something he hadn't mentioned — extra calamari, no charge."

### Honest, not hype.
We don't do "MUST-VISIT!!" energy. We do "if you're already in the
area, this is worth it" or "the Caesar was better than I expected,
the steak was a 6/10."

### Family first, but not saccharine.
The family is woven in naturally — "the kids burned through three
sides of fries before our entrees came" — not "we LOVE making
memories together!" Avoid Instagram-caption emotional voice.

### Write like a long Yelp review by someone who can write.
A great restaurant review is about 400–700 words. It tells a small
story. It has logistics info families care about (parking, kid menu,
wait time, stroller, reservations). It has at least one moment that
feels like Brandon was actually there.

### Disappointments handled with warmth.
"We wanted to love it" is the right energy when something falls
short. Not mean, not snarky, not unwilling to say it was bad. Always
give context: "we were two hours into a long drive and probably
hangrier than we should have been."

### "We" by default, "I" when it's about Brandon's individual
experience or expertise. The Johnsons are a unit — that's a feature
of the brand.

## 4. Phrasing patterns to avoid

- ❌ "MUST-VISIT" / "MUST-TRY" / "BEST EVER"
- ❌ "Hidden gem" (overused; use only if Brandon explicitly says so)
- ❌ "Foodie" (cringe)
- ❌ "This place will blow your mind"
- ❌ "Y'all need to try this"
- ❌ "Living my best life" / Instagram-caption energy
- ❌ "Sommelier," "concierge," "bespoke," "curated excellence,"
  "uncompromising palate" — all luxury-critic placeholder language
  from the reference design that crept into v0 of the site
- ❌ Em-dash–heavy editorial style if it feels like a magazine piece.
  Brandon is a guy talking, not Vogue.
- ❌ Travel-blog clichés: "wanderlust," "epic," "soul-stirring,"
  "transformative experience"
- ❌ Reviewing-AI tells: "nestled in," "boasts," "this gem of a
  restaurant," "perfect blend of"

- **No identifying kids by full name** unless Brandon explicitly

  or first name only if Brandon has signed off.
- **Strip geotags from every photo that includes the kids** before
  publishing. Always. No exceptions. See `/ceo/photos.md`.
- **No photos that clearly tag a kid at a specific school, friend's
  home, or recurring activity.** Restaurants, hotels, theme parks,
  airports — fine. Schools, daily routines — never.

- Say "we loved the tiramisu" if Brandon never told us they had
  the tiramisu.

  cruelty is not the brand.
- Take a comp without disclosing it. (And: agent doesn't accept

- Use "MUST VISIT!" anywhere on the site. Anywhere.
