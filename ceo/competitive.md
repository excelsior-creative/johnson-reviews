# Competitive landscape

Lightweight. Update when we learn something; don't try to
map the whole internet.

## Direct overlap (family travel reviewer blogs)

- **TravelingMom** — traveling family network, mostly mom-
  led. Very listicle-heavy, affiliate-dense. Template to
  beat on specificity.
- **FamilyVacationist** — staff-written, SEO-forward. Good
  structure, weak personal voice. Our edge.
- **Trekaroo** — Disney / themepark specialist. Thorough
  logistics. Strong on kid-angle, worth studying for
  section structure.
- **Y Travel Blog** — Aussie couple, long-form, photo-
  heavy. Design-aspirational.
- **Have Baby Will Travel** — very narrow (infants +
  toddlers). Relevant niche but tight audience.

## Indirect overlap (restaurant review blogs)

- **Eater LA / Eater Vegas / Eater SD** — editorial, not
  personal brand. We are the anti-Eater: personal, honest,
  family-focused, local.
- **YelpElite locals** — real user reviews, no SEO moat.
  Brandon's voice + SEO + family angle is the differentiator.
- **TripAdvisor** — aggregator, commodity. Not a
  competitor for voice or trust.

## Who Brandon is NOT (creative-direction guardrail)

- Not Bon Appétit / New York Times critic voice
- Not Eater LA editorial voice
- Not a Disney Adult influencer with a catchphrase
- Not an Instagram "family of 6 on a schoolbus" content farm
- Not an affiliate-site SEO rewriter

## What to study (monthly)

- Look at 3 top-ranking "family restaurants [OC city]" search
  results. What's in their schema, their header structure,
  their internal linking? What specific things do families
  care about that they *don't* cover?
- Look at 3 top-ranking "Disneyland with toddlers" results.
  Same audit.
- Save useful findings in `experiments.md` or as ADRs.
