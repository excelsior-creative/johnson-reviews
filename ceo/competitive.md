# Competitive landscape

Who we benchmark against. Short list — fill out as we learn.

## Direct family-travel benchmarks

- **The Bucket List Family** — global, polished, video-first, kid
  faces visible. Different brand, but a useful reference for what
  family-travel content can become at scale.
- **Travel Mamas** — restaurant + family travel, mostly listicles.
  Voice gets blog-generic. We can win on specificity.
- **Trekaroo** (now defunct) — was strong on family-friendly
  reviews. Their archive is still indexable; their gap is our
  opportunity in SoCal.
- **Have Baby Will Travel** — strong destination guides, weak on
  individual restaurant reviews.

## Direct restaurant-review benchmarks

- **Eater LA** — professional voice, no family lens, very local.
  Best-in-class for lists ("16 Italian restaurants in Orange
  County"). We can complement, not compete.
- **OC Weekly** restaurant section (legacy archive) — gone but
  still ranks. SoCal SEO opportunity.
- **The Infatuation** — opinionated, voice-heavy, no family lens.
  Closest brand-voice cousin if family lens were swapped out.

## Hyper-local

- **OC Mom Collective / OC Mom Activities** — listicle-heavy,
  family-first, weak on photo quality and review depth. Easy bar.
- **Visit Anaheim / Visit Dana Point / Visit Laguna Beach** —
  partner / DMO content, not editorial. Useful as backlink targets.

## What we're betting we can do better

1. **Voice.** Most family-travel sites read like SEO factories.
   Brandon-voice reviews stand out instantly.
2. **Editorial design.** Most family-travel blogs are bright,
   childlike, cluttered. The dark-luxury aesthetic is a brand
   moat — we look like a magazine, not a Mommy blog.
3. **Honest negative reviews.** Most family-travel content is
   relentlessly upbeat. Constructive critical reviews build trust
   that travel-positive reviews never can on their own.
4. **Local depth, then breadth.** Owning OC / SD / LA /
   Vegas before reaching for international gives us a defensible
   position.
